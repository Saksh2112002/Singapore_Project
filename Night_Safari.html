🌙 Night Safari Singapore

The Night Safari is the world’s first nocturnal wildlife park, offering an after‑dark adventure through six geographical zones. Board the guided tram or explore walking trails to see over 900 animals — from elusive leopards to majestic Asian elephants — in their nighttime habitats.

Highlights

  • Tram Safari Journey:


    📍 Location: Night Safari, 80 Mandai Lake Road, Singapore 729826
    🚋 Overview: The Tram Safari Journey is the signature experience of Night Safari, offering a guided ride through 7 geographical zones of the world. Visitors can spot free-roaming animals under soft moonlight and listen to live commentary.
    🦁 What You’ll See:
    • Himalayan Tahr and Bharal on rugged terrain
    • Malayan Tapirs and Asian Elephants
    • Spotted Hyenas and Striped Hyenas
    • Lions, Bongos, and Indian Gaur
    🚇 How to Get There: MRT to Khatib Station (NS14), then take Mandai Khatib Shuttle or bus 138
    🕐 Operating Hours: Daily from 7:15 PM – 12:00 AM (last entry 11:15 PM)
    🌟 Tips:
    • Go early for the first tram ride to avoid queues
    • Don’t use flash photography — it disturbs animals
    • Use the live commentary to learn fun animal facts

  • Walking Trails:


    📍 Location: Night Safari, 80 Mandai Lake Road, Singapore 729826
    🌳 Overview: In addition to the tram ride, Night Safari features four immersive walking trails that bring you even closer to nocturnal wildlife in lush, naturalistic settings.
    🦉 Key Trails:
    • Leopard Trail: Spot stealthy leopards, clouded leopards, and flying squirrels in action.
    • Fishing Cat Trail: Watch fishing cats hunt and marvel at wetland creatures like otters and civets.
    • East Lodge Trail: Encounter Malayan tigers and red river hogs.
    • Wallaby Trail: Stroll through an Australian habitat with wallabies hopping freely.
    🚇 How to Get There: MRT to Khatib (NS14), then Mandai Khatib Shuttle or Bus 138
    🕐 Trail Hours: Daily from 7:15 PM to 12:00 AM (last entry: 11:15 PM)
    🌟 Tips:
    • Bring quiet shoes – some animals are nearby and easily startled.
    • Stay silent and look carefully – many creatures are camouflaged.
    • Great for photography lovers – low-light trail lighting creates a magical ambiance.

  • Creatures of the Night Show:


    📍 Location: Amphitheatre, Night Safari, 80 Mandai Lake Road, Singapore 729826
    🎭 Overview: The "Creatures of the Night" is a 25-minute interactive show that spotlights the unique behaviors and talents of nocturnal animals. Hosted by experienced keepers, the show features live demonstrations and unexpected animal appearances.
    🦝 What You’ll See:
    • Otters showing off their recycling skills
    • Raccoons and binturongs in clever routines
    • Spotted hyenas crushing bones with powerful jaws
    • Snakes, owls, and civets up close with audience interaction
    🚇 How to Get There: MRT to Khatib (NS14), then Mandai Khatib Shuttle or Bus 138
    🕐 Showtimes: Daily at 7:30 PM, 8:30 PM, and 9:30 PM (subject to weather)
    🌟 Tips:
    • Arrive 15–20 minutes early for good seats—it's free seating and fills up fast.
    • No flash photography—respect the animals' comfort.
    • Sit near the front for possible interaction with the animals!

  • Thumbuakar Fire Show:


    📍 Location: Entrance Courtyard, Night Safari, 80 Mandai Lake Road, Singapore 729826
    🔥 Overview: The Thumbuakar Fire Show is a thrilling prelude to the Night Safari adventure, performed by a group of talented fire dancers. This high-energy tribal performance combines flame-spitting, acrobatics, and rhythmic drumming to create a jaw-dropping experience.
    🎪 What to Expect:
    • Fire-breathing and flame juggling
    • Tribal warrior-themed choreography and music
    • Energetic group routines and crowd interaction
    🚇 How to Get There: MRT to Khatib (NS14), then take Mandai Khatib Shuttle or Bus 138
    🕐 Showtimes: Daily at 6:45 PM, 8:00 PM, and 9:00 PM (each show is ~7 minutes)
    🌟 Tips:
    • Catch this before entering the park—it happens just outside the ticket gate.
    • Great for photo ops—just remember to turn off your flash.
    • Best viewing spot is center stage near the courtyard benches.

Dining Options

  • Ulu Ulu Safari Restaurant: Asian buffet and local delights in a rustic kampong setting.
  • Chang Chawang "Thai Street Food": Quick bites like pad Thai and mango sticky rice.
  • New Zealand Natural: Ice cream and smoothies for a sweet treat post‑tour.

📍 Location & How to Get There

Address: 80 Mandai Lake Road, Singapore 729826 (next to Singapore Zoo). From the city, take MRT to Khatib (NS14) then Mandai Khatib Shuttle (15 min). Or bus 927/138 to the zoo complex.

🕐 Opening Hours

7:15 PM – 12:00 AM daily (last entry 11:15 PM). Tram queues close 11:00 PM.

Visitor Tips

  • Buy e‑tickets in advance for timed entry slots.
  • Arrive before 7 PM to catch the first fire show and avoid long tram queues.
  • No flash photography — it startles the animals.
  • Wear comfortable walking shoes and carry insect repellent.
