🏛️ National Museum of Singapore

The National Museum of Singapore is the oldest museum in the country, first established in 1887. It’s a cultural and historical landmark, showcasing Singapore’s journey from past to present through engaging exhibits and immersive galleries.

📍 Location: 93 Stamford Road, Singapore 178897

🕐 Opening Hours: Daily from 10:00 AM to 7:00 PM (last admission 6:30 PM)

🎟️ Admission:

  • Free for Singaporeans & Permanent Residents
  • $15 for Adults (Standard ticket)
  • $10 for Students & Seniors

🖼️ Highlights:

  • Singapore History Gallery:


    📍 Location: Level 1, National Museum of Singapore, 93 Stamford Road, Singapore 178897
    🏛️ Overview: This flagship gallery offers a fascinating journey through Singapore’s history, from its 14th-century origins to independence and modern success.
    🖼️ Key Highlights:
    • Artifacts from ancient Singapore (Temasek era)
    • British colonial rule and World War II exhibits
    • Multimedia and immersive storytelling experiences
    • Sections on the Japanese Occupation and Independence
    🎧 Interactive Features:
    • Touchscreen panels and digital timelines
    • Free audio guide via the National Museum app
    • Augmented reality views of historical events
    🕐 Opening Hours: 10:00 AM – 7:00 PM daily (last entry at 6:30 PM)
    🌟 Tips:
    • Start your museum visit here for historical context
    • Allow 60–90 minutes for a full walkthrough
    • Photography is allowed without flash

  • Life in Singapore Galleries:


    📍 Location: Level 2, National Museum of Singapore, 93 Stamford Road, Singapore 178897
    🏙️ Overview: The Life in Singapore Galleries explore everyday living from the late 1800s to the modern era, showcasing how Singaporeans have worked, played, and celebrated through the decades.
    🖼️ Key Themes:
    • Early migration and multicultural communities
    • Domestic life in kampongs and HDB flats
    • Singapore’s street food, trades, and childhood pastimes
    • Music, fashion, and entertainment from the '50s to today
    🧠 Experience & Interaction:
    • Replica kitchens, salons, and coffee shops from different eras
    • Interactive audio stations and video clips
    • Nostalgic items like old toys, radios, and furniture
    🕐 Opening Hours: 10:00 AM – 7:00 PM daily (last admission 6:30 PM)
    🌟 Tips:
    • Perfect for visitors interested in Singapore's cultural identity
    • Great for families and older generations — lots of nostalgia
    • Check for special photo booths and storytelling events

  • Glass Rotunda: Story of the Forest


    📍 Location: Level 2, National Museum of Singapore, 93 Stamford Road, Singapore 178897
    🏞️ Overview: The Glass Rotunda is a stunning immersive installation that blends art, nature, and technology. It houses **“Story of the Forest,”** a digital interpretation of the museum’s historic natural history drawings, brought to life by Japanese art collective teamLab.
    🌳 What You'll Experience:
    • Enter a dark tunnel that opens up into a 360° dome of animated rainforest
    • Spot over 60 species of flora and fauna as they move and react around you
    • See digital animals leap across walls, trees sway, and the weather shift in real time
    📸 Highlights:
    • Perfect photo spot with magical visuals and ambient music
    • Great for both kids and adults — it's educational and immersive
    • Use the museum’s AR app to scan and learn more about each species
    🕐 Opening Hours: 10:00 AM – 7:00 PM (last admission at 6:15 PM)
    🌟 Tips:
    • Visit early or late for smaller crowds and better photo opportunities
    • Don’t rush through — take your time to observe subtle animations
    • Perfect ending after the “Life in Singapore” galleries

  • Interactive Exhibits & Family Zones


    📍 Location: Various galleries & Basement Level, National Museum of Singapore
    🧒 Overview: Perfect for families traveling with kids, the museum offers hands-on exhibits, engaging play areas, and family-friendly activities designed to make learning history fun.
    🎮 What You’ll Find:
    • Interactive Touchscreens: Dive into digital storytelling and explore historical artifacts through games and videos.
    • Storytelling Sessions: Live and recorded sessions in cozy corners with cushions and props for younger visitors.
    • DIY Stations: Creative craft booths where children can make bookmarks, sketches, or postcards inspired by history.
    🧩 Family Zones & Kids Areas:
    • “Play @ NMS”: A vibrant, colorful space for imaginative play, role-play exhibits, and sensory games.
    • Reading Nooks: With books, beanbags, and museum-themed puzzles.
    • Photo Booths: Dress up in period costumes and take family portraits with historic Singapore backdrops.
    🎟️ Admission: Included with general entry. Some workshops may require advance registration.
    🕐 Recommended Time: 1–2 hours for family-focused exploration
    🌟 Tips:
    • Plan your visit around workshop timings listed at the museum info desk.
    • Bring a camera — the kids' zones are adorable and colorful!
    • Best time to visit: weekday mornings for fewer crowds.

🚇 How to Get There:
MRT: Bras Basah (Circle Line, CC2) or Dhoby Ghaut (NS24/NE6/CC1) – a 5-minute walk.

☕ Nearby Spots:

  • Fort Canning Park for a relaxing stroll
  • SMU & Singapore Art Museum (SAM)
  • National Library & Food Junction nearby

🌟 Tips:

  • Use the museum’s mobile app for interactive guides
  • Visit in the morning to avoid crowds
  • Check out temporary exhibitions and night festivals
