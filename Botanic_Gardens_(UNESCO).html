🌿 Singapore Botanic Gardens (UNESCO)

The Singapore Botanic Gardens, established in 1859, is the country's first and only UNESCO World Heritage Site. It's a peaceful, lush sanctuary in the heart of the city where nature, history, and culture blend seamlessly. A favorite for locals and tourists alike, it spans over 82 hectares.

🌸 Highlights

  • National Orchid Garden:


    📍 Location: Singapore Botanic Gardens, 1 Cluny Rd, Singapore 259569
    🏙️ Overview: Located within the UNESCO-listed Singapore Botanic Gardens, this garden showcases over 1,000 orchid species and 2,000 hybrids in a beautifully landscaped setting.
    🌸 What You’ll Find:
    • Orchid Collections: VIP orchids named after global figures
    • Cool House: Mist-filled glasshouse with rare mountain orchids
    • Tropical Montane Orchidetum: Elevated walkway & unique orchids
    • Heritage Orchid Garden: Preserved hybrids from the early 1900s
    🚇 How to Get There: MRT Downtown Line → Botanic Gardens Station (DT9/CC19), Exit A, 15-minute walk
    🕐 Opening Hours: Daily, 8:30 AM – 7:00 PM (last entry 6 PM)
    🎫 Admission: Adults: SGD 5 | Seniors (60+): SGD 1 | Children under 12: Free
    🌟 Tips:
    • Best visited in the morning or late afternoon
    • Bring a camera—orchid displays are stunning year-round
    • Visit the gift shop for unique orchid-themed souvenirs

  • Rainforest Trail:


    📍 Location: Near Tyersall Entrance, Singapore Botanic Gardens, 1 Cluny Road, Singapore 259569
    🌳 Overview: The Rainforest Trail is a lush, shaded path winding through a dense patch of preserved primary rainforest. It showcases Singapore’s native flora and the biodiversity of tropical forests in a peaceful, educational setting.
    🌱 What You’ll Find:
    • Over 300 species of flora native to the region
    • Ancient trees towering over the canopy
    • Educational signboards detailing plant species and ecology
    • Quiet, cooling path ideal for nature walks
    🚇 How to Get There: MRT Downtown Line → Botanic Gardens Station (DT9/CC19), Exit A, follow signs to Tyersall Entrance
    🕐 Opening Hours: Daily from 5 AM to 12 Midnight (same as Singapore Botanic Gardens)
    💡 Tips:
    • Wear comfortable walking shoes — trail paths can be slightly uneven
    • Best enjoyed early morning or late evening for cooler temperatures
    • Bring insect repellent and water for your walk

  • Swan Lake:


    📍 Location: Near the Tanglin Gate entrance, Singapore Botanic Gardens, 1 Cluny Road, Singapore 259569
    🦢 Overview: Swan Lake is one of the oldest ornamental water features in Singapore and a tranquil gem within the Botanic Gardens. It features a beautiful fountain and is home to elegant mute swans from Amsterdam.
    🌿 What You’ll Find:
    • Peaceful pond with swans gliding gracefully across the water
    • Iconic white swan sculpture in the center of the lake
    • Benches and shaded areas for relaxation and photography
    • Lush surroundings with flowering trees and water plants
    🚇 How to Get There: MRT Downtown Line → Botanic Gardens Station (DT9/CC19), walk toward Tanglin Gate
    🕐 Opening Hours: Daily from 5 AM to 12 Midnight
    💡 Tips:
    • Great for peaceful morning strolls or romantic sunset walks
    • Bring a camera — perfect spot for nature and wildlife photography
    • Look out for monitor lizards and turtles in the water

  • Jacob Ballas Children’s Garden:


    📍 Location: 481 Bukit Timah Road, Singapore 259769 (adjacent to the main Singapore Botanic Gardens)
    🌱 Overview: The first garden in Asia dedicated to children, Jacob Ballas Children’s Garden is designed to nurture a love for nature through play and learning. It’s a fun, safe, and interactive space where kids can explore, discover, and grow.
    🎡 Attractions & Highlights:
    • Adventure play zones with bridges, treehouses, and slides
    • Mini farming and gardening zones
    • Water play area for cooling off
    • Interactive exhibits on photosynthesis and plant life
    • Maze, sand play, and sensory trails
    👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Ideal For:
    • Families with children aged 14 and below
    • Educational visits and school excursions
    🚇 How to Get There: MRT Downtown Line → Botanic Gardens Station (DT9/CC19), walk toward Bukit Timah Gate
    🕐 Opening Hours: 8 AM – 7 PM (Last entry at 6:30 PM) | Closed on Mondays (except PH)
    💡 Tips:
    • Admission is free for all children and accompanying adults
    • Bring extra clothes for water play and sunscreen
    • No entry for adults unless accompanied by a child

  • Ginger Garden:


    📍 Location: Near the Tanglin Gate, Singapore Botanic Gardens, 1 Cluny Road, Singapore 259569
    🌿 Overview: The Ginger Garden is a lush, tropical paradise featuring over 250 species of gingers and related plants. It’s an aromatic, shaded retreat perfect for a relaxing stroll surrounded by vibrant flowers and unique foliage.
    🌺 Highlights:
    • Massive collection of ornamental gingers from around the world
    • Waterfall feature and stream running through the garden
    • Canopy walk with elevated wooden paths for scenic views
    • Night-blooming plants like torch gingers and wild bananas
    📸 Instagram-worthy Spots:
    • Walk-through waterfall cave with photo-friendly lighting
    • Ginger-shaped pavilion surrounded by lush greenery
    🚇 How to Get There: MRT Circle Line → Botanic Gardens Station (CC19/DT9), enter via Tanglin Gate
    🕐 Opening Hours: 5 AM – 12 Midnight daily
    💡 Tips:
    • Best visited in the morning for cooler temperatures
    • Great for nature photography and peaceful picnics
    • Wear non-slip shoes if exploring near the waterfall

📍 Location

1 Cluny Road, Singapore 259569

🚇 How to Get There

Take the Downtown Line or Circle Line MRT → Alight at Botanic Gardens Station (DT9/CC19)

🕐 Opening Hours

5:00 AM to 12:00 Midnight daily (free entry)

National Orchid Garden: 8:30 AM – 7:00 PM (last entry: 6 PM, entry fees apply)

🌟 Visitor Tips

  • Wear comfortable walking shoes and bring water.
  • Visit early morning or late afternoon to avoid heat.
  • Use the Eco Lake or Tanglin Gate for scenic entrances.
  • Great picnic spot! Bring light snacks or enjoy nearby cafes.
